What I Do

Security

I care deeply about security because it is both a professional responsibility and a broader societal imperative. In the tech industry, ensuring robust security measures is crucial for protecting sensitive data, maintaining system integrity, and building trust with users and clients. However, security extends beyond individual organizations—it plays a critical role in safeguarding societal infrastructure, healthcare systems, and even national security from malicious attacks. By prioritizing security, I not only help protect the organizations I work with but also contribute to the larger goal of creating a safer, more resilient digital world for everyone.

Web Development

Web development was the first "part" of the computer science I learned through my university courses. It was also the first work I got as I was able to use what I had learnt at school to create a professional website for the online gaming group Imperial Black Legion(IBL). This interest did in many ways lead me down the path towards cybersecurity, but I maintain an interest for this part of computer science.

Admin

In my spare time the last 7 years I have been doing server admin for the online gaming community IBL. Through this work I have become experienced in how to interact and communicate clearly with server hosts and other potential outside parties that we rent services from. In addition, I have also had to teach myself how to work around any potential overhead-conflicts that might arise when operating multiple technologies from different manufacturers/developers in the same project.

Management

Along with doing server admin for the IBL I have also run multiple staff departments within the organization. To clarify, the group has roughly 750 members. A group of this size, even an online one, requires a good bit of organization to function properly. As such we have multiple staff departments that help with the day to day running of the unit. The two departments I have overseen are the Development department and the Community Operations department. The latter is a department that ensured that any games played by the community would be of high enough quality to ensure that the IBL standard was kept. The former is the department that works on developing new content (mods) for the group to use in the game we play. Here I got to experience what it is like to start a development team from scratch and then manage said team so that we reach the goals we set for our ongoing project.
